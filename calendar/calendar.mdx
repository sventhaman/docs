Every client workspace can have its own appointment calendar. Once it's on, the client's agents can book, reschedule, and cancel appointments during voice and chat conversations, you can manage appointments by hand, and customers can book themselves from a shareable booking page. The calendar belongs to the **client**, so any agent assigned to that client books into the same calendar.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Calendar section">
    In the left sidebar, go to **Calendar** and pick the client you want a calendar for. The list shows each client and whether its calendar is on.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Turn the calendar on">
    Open **Settings** for that client and toggle the calendar **Enabled**. Enabling it automatically wires the booking tools into that client's agents — no extra setup.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your booking rules">
    Under **Booking**, choose:
    - **Timezone** — all appointment times are shown and booked in this timezone.
    - **Appointment length** — how long each slot is.
    - **Buffer** — gap kept between appointments.
    - **Reminder email** — when to email the attendee before their appointment (off, 1h, 3h, 24h, or 48h).
    - **Minimum notice** — how far in advance a self-serve booking must be made.
    - **Booking window** — how far ahead customers can book.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set working hours">
    Under **Working hours**, switch each day on or off and set its open/close times. Customers and agents can only book within these hours.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose who gets notified">
    Under **Notifications**, add one or more **business notification emails** (your client's front desk, owner, etc.), and choose whether to **email the recipient** and which events (new booking, reschedule, cancellation) send mail. Emails come from your agency's branded sender.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing appointments

Open **Calendar** for a client to see a month view of upcoming appointments. Click any day to add an appointment, or click an existing one to edit its time, reschedule it, or cancel it. Days with several appointments show a **+N more** link that opens the full day.

When you add an appointment by hand, you can tick **Send a confirmation email** to notify the attendee — this overrides the calendar's default, so it sends even if recipient emails are normally off.

<Note>
  Confirmation emails include a calendar invite (`.ics`) so the attendee can add the appointment to their own calendar in one click.
</Note>

## Booking by agent

When the calendar is on, the client's agents can handle scheduling on a call or chat automatically — checking open times, booking, rescheduling, and cancelling. Just tell the agent (in its system prompt) that it can manage appointments; the tools are already attached.

## Let customers book themselves

You can also share a public booking page or embed it on the client's website — see [The booking page](/calendar/booking-page).
